## Sample press angles

For pitching journalists / podcasters. Pick the angle that fits the publication.

### Angle 1: "Anti-AI meal planner"
> Most meal-planning apps lean on AI to generate your week. Replanish doesn't. Their weekly drop is curated — 126 recipes from real food blogs, hand-picked, free for everyone. **The argument:** AI is at its best for the daily-decision moments ("swap tonight's dinner"), not Sunday-batch generation, and they're betting curated content + per-meal AI is more reliable than per-user weekly AI plans.

### Angle 2: "The shopping list is the business model"
> Replanish is free because shopping cart affiliate is the long-term revenue lever. Their entire architecture — offline-first lists, real-time sync, deduped baskets, link-first recipes — is built around making the shopping list the thing that earns money, not the meal planner.

### Angle 3: "Family software for the US"
> Most family / household coordination tools are either calendar add-ons or single-purpose (chores OR meals OR events). Replanish bundles meals + shopping + events + chores + activities into one app, scoped to a "circle" (your household, your friend group, your event group). The bet: families don't want five apps for five problems.

### Angle 4: "Recipe rights done right"
> Recipe sites have spent years fighting AI scrapers. Replanish takes a different posture: link-first to the original source, ingredient lists only (factual, not copyrightable per *Feist v. Rural*), public attribution, opt-out within 7 days. **The argument:** there's a model that respects content owners *and* surfaces their work to busy users.
